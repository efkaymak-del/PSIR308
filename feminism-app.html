The Gender Lens Analyzer

PSIR 308 · Feminism · Kymlicka Chapter 9

Every policy and social arrangement looks different depending on which feminist theory you use to examine it.

Liberal feminism asks whether rights and opportunities are extended equally. Radical feminism (MacKinnon) asks whether the arrangement is built on a structure of male dominance. Socialist feminism asks whether unpaid gendered labour subsidizes the system. Justice-in-the-family (Okin) asks whether Rawls's own principles are applied consistently to the household.

In this simulation, you will examine ten social arrangements. For each one, you will read what all four feminist lenses see — and then choose which lens you find most revealing for that case.

There are no right answers. But your pattern of choices will reveal which feminist theoretical intuitions you gravitate toward — and what that says about how you understand gender, power, and justice.
