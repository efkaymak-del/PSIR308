The Pre-Political Lens

PSIR 308 · Critical Theory · Beauvoir & Fanon

Every political theory you have studied this semester begins with subjects — individuals who have preferences, identities, rights, and the capacity to choose. Justice is then about how to treat these subjects fairly.

Beauvoir and Fanon ask a prior question: how were those subjects made?

The "pre-political" is everything that shapes who you are before you enter the political arena — gendered socialization, racial construction, colonial history, cultural norms, internalized hierarchies. These forces determine what you believe you deserve, what you can imagine for yourself, and whether you are recognized as fully human.

In this simulation, you will examine eight scenarios. For each one, you will first identify what a liberal theory sees (rights, fairness, distribution). Then you will identify what the pre-political lens reveals — what Beauvoir or Fanon would see that liberalism misses. Finally, you will judge whether the pre-political critique changes the verdict or merely deepens the liberal analysis.

Your pattern of judgments will reveal how far you think political philosophy needs to go beneath the surface.
