BLOCK 2 · ~20 minutes

The Theories on Trial

Three theories. Beauvoir and Fanon are the prosecutors.

Theory 1 — Rawls

Rawls puts you behind the veil of ignorance to design just institutions.

Beauvoir says the person behind the veil has already been gendered — their imagination of what is possible has been shaped before they arrive.

Fanon says the veil assumes everyone behind it is recognized as rational and human, which the colonial system denies.

Does the veil of ignorance survive this critique?

Push: "needs modification" vs. "fundamentally broken"

Theory 2 — Nozick

Nozick says you own yourself.

Fanon says the colonized person does not own themselves — their body and identity have been claimed by the colonial system.

Beauvoir says women's selves have been constructed for them.

Is self-ownership a starting point or a conclusion?

Press defenders: how does self-ownership apply to someone whose subjectivity was constructed by the system?

Theory 3 — MacKinnon

MacKinnon argued that gender inequality is domination, not discrimination.

Beauvoir goes further — the category "woman" itself is constructed.

Does Beauvoir strengthen MacKinnon or undermine her?

If "woman" is a construction, who is MacKinnon fighting for?

Let the tension sit. If intersectionality or essentialism comes up, follow it.

The Verdict

Did any of these theories survive the pre-political critique intact?

The Classroom Itself

Scenario 8

You are studying Beauvoir and Fanon in English, at a university modelled on Western higher education, in a territory shaped by colonialism and partition.

Fanon said that the university is the most refined instrument of colonial subject-construction.

Is this course an act of liberation or an act of assimilation — or both?

Defend Your Position

defend that position to someone who chose "the pre-political critique changes the verdict."

Closing

Your theoretical autobiography asks where your thinking started, how it changed, and where it stands now.

Today's question — whether this course constructed you or liberated you — is one you may want to sit with as you write.

You do not have to answer it. But you should not pretend it does not exist.
